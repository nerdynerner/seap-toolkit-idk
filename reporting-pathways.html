Making reporting easier to navigate

Today: many official channels, no obvious route

Different offices have valid roles, but the overall system presents as scattered, overlapping, and difficult for frontline contacts to navigate.

There's no shortage of official channels, at least 8 offices touch this in some way. The problem is that almost nobody uses them. Data shows students, staff, and grad students defaulting instead to informal contacts: department chairs, instructors, academic advisors, and the SSE, most of whom don't know where to redirect them.

Expand anonymized findings from student and staff feedback

One student described the entire complaint process as an illusion that further adds to student burnout and the mental health crisis.

A student who complained to a Psychology department chair about an instructor was simply told to speak to the professor directly, despite expressing fear about doing so.

Students who brought concerns to the SSE reported being ignored or told the office was focused on other administrative matters.

Reporting information was described as somewhat obscured on U of A websites, with students often directed back to the same confusing links.

Academic advisors say they're often the first point of contact for students in distress but feel they lack training for human rights issues, so they end up outsourcing the problem rather than referring it anywhere specific.

Grad students describe reporting as “always much harder” due to power imbalance with supervisors. OSDHR and the Vice-Provost, Access, Community and Belonging were almost never named or recognized in feedback, despite being the official channels.
